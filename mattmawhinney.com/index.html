About Me

My name is Matt Mawhinney, and I am a web developer in training. I already know a little bit of Ruby, HTML, CSS, and jQuery, and from July through September I will be at Dev Bootcamp in Chicago where I will learn Rails, JavaScript, how to work in a professional development environment, and much more!


For the past two years, I was living and working in sunny Austin, Texas where I simultanesouly caught the tech bug, the music bug, and the BBQ bug. After winning tickets to the Evernote Trunk Conference last August and TEDxAustin in February of this year along with various adventurues in collaborative consumption, I decided I needed to be smack-dab in the middle of the tech industry, solving problems, building products, and putting a dent in the universe.


At heart, I am a social science nerd. I studied history as an undergrad at the University of Pennsylvania, and I have a master's degree in public policy from George Mason University. Thus far in my career, I've worked primarily in the government and consulting spaces, doing stints with the US Agency for International Development in Morocco, the US Department of Commerce in Washington, and most recently Strategic Forecasting in Austin. I view becoming a programmer not as a step away from the social sciences and civic engagment but as a way to tackle important commercial and societal challenges with a different set of tools. No matter what I end up doing in my day-to-day coding jobs, I definitely see more events like The Natioanl Civic Day of Hacking in my future.


When I am not learning to code or keeping up with the news, I like to swim, cook, play with my baby niece Fara, and occasionally do improv commedy.
